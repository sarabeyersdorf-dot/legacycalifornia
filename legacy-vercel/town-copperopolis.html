Field notes · § I

A copper town
that became a lake town.

Copperopolis was a mining town first — one of the largest copper deposits in the West, dug hard in the 1860s and again during both World Wars. Then the mines closed, the foothills emptied, and the town went quiet for thirty years.

In 1957 the Tri-Dam Project finished damming the Stanislaus River and Lake Tulloch was born — a long, narrow reservoir with fifty-five miles of shoreline, most of it framed by Copperopolis hillsides. By the 1980s the town was a lake town. By the 2000s it was also a golf town, when the resort now known as Copper Valley opened its Greg Norman-designed course south of the lake.

Today, Copperopolis is the warmest, lowest-elevation, and most resort-shaped town in the survey. Lake-front estates, dock-out properties, golf-community homes, big-sky rural parcels — the housing stock is wider than anywhere else in the region.

    A note from your agent · § VI
    SC Sara Cooper · Owner-Broker

    A few words on Copperopolis —

    Of all the towns in this survey, Copperopolis is the one I send Bay Area buyers to most often. The math is unfussy: an hour and forty-five minutes from the East Bay, dock-out properties that would cost three million on Tahoe selling at half the number, a Greg Norman golf course nobody east of Stockton has heard of.

    The trade-off is that this is a resort town, not a historic Main Street town. The "downtown" is a mock-historic square built in 2007. Most of the residential stock is HOA. The schools are smaller, the medical center is fifteen minutes away in Sonora. Be honest with yourself about which of those things actually matter.

    If you want big water, big sky, mild winters, and a dock fifty feet from your kitchen — Copperopolis is the answer. Each community is its own micro-world. I know the dock owners on most of them.

    — Sara
