The Long Version

From the press room
to the foothills.

Sara's first career had nothing to do with square footage and everything to do with narrative. For more than thirty years she worked as a global publicist in electronic and dance music — the person who built an artist's reputation, shaped how the world saw them, and earned the trust of people who had every reason to be guarded. You learn things there that no licensing exam covers.

You learn that nothing sells itself — and that the truth, told with care, always lands harder than hype. You learn to find the real story underneath the noise, to earn trust before you ask for it, and to represent someone so faithfully that they recognize themselves in the result. Thirty years of that doesn't leave you — it becomes how you see everything.

"Publicity and real estate are the same craft. You're never selling the thing. You're telling its story honestly enough that the right person feels it."

So when Sara turned that sensibility toward real estate, she didn't arrive as a typical agent with a typical pitch. She arrived as someone who already knew how to represent people well — how to hear what a buyer is really after underneath the bed-and-bath count, and how to present a seller's home so its story lands the moment you walk in.

That's the whole idea behind Legacy Properties, the boutique Gold Country brokerage she owns and runs. Small on purpose. Personal by design. A brokerage where every listing gets the attention of the person whose name is on the door — not a handoff to a junior whose name you'll never learn.

The brand carries the look of the life that built it: the warm, analog romance of a vintage microphone — chrome and ribbon and stage light — because the through-line of Sara's whole career has been the same. Give people a voice. Help them tell the story they came to tell. Every home has a story isn't a tagline she focus-grouped. It's the only way she's ever known how to work.

The Philosophy

Story-driven luxury.
Every home has a story.

Luxury, to Sara, was never about the highest price per square foot. It's about the homes that hold a narrative — the 1880s cottage with a hundred winters in its beams, the lake place where three generations learned to swim, the vineyard estate waiting for its next chapter. Her job is to find the buyer who deserves that story, and to tell it well enough that they recognize it as theirs.
