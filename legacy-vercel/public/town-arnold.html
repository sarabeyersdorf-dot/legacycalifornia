Field notes · § I

A cabin town that
never quite grew up.

Eighteen miles east of Murphys, the road climbs to nearly four thousand feet into sugar pines and Jeffrey pines and the kind of silence Bay Area people drive five hours for.

Arnold doesn't have a Main Street so much as a string of cabins, a market, a coffee shop, a hardware store, and a tavern — laid out along Highway 4 like beads on a single thread. That's the whole town. That's the point.

It's been a cabin town since the 1920s, when San Joaquin Valley families built summer houses up the canyon to escape the heat. Most of those cabins are still standing, still owned by the same families, still rented out the weekend the kids graduate.

Who Arnold is for —

Closer to the grandkids, four seasons, a porch big enough for the dogs. Down the hill to Murphys when the cooking mood strikes.

A note from your agent · § VI
SC Sara Cooper · Owner-Broker

A few words on Arnold —

Most of Arnold's market is cabins, second homes, and increasingly, full-time residents who used to come up on weekends and decided to stop leaving. That last group is the fastest-growing share of buyers I've worked with in the past two years. Fiber-to-the-home rolled out in 2023 and quietly changed the math.

If you're looking up here, the questions worth asking are about snow load, septic age, fire setbacks, and the road grade in February. I can help you ask them.

— Sara
