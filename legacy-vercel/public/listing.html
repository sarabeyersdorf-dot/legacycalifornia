Overview · Written by Sara

A property for people who came up for the weekend
and never quite left.

Vineyard Ridge sits high on a sun-soft southwestern slope above Sutter Creek — four and a half acres of decomposed granite and old oak, ready to be planted, and a 1998 estate home built when local craftsmen still finished trim by hand.

The house is generous but unfussy. A chef's kitchen with two ovens and a six-burner range opens onto a great room anchored by a Roman-clay fireplace and a wall of glass that takes the valley straight through to the foothills. The primary suite occupies its own wing — French doors to a private courtyard, a stone-tiled walk-in shower, and a soaking tub set under a north-facing clerestory.

Downstairs: a wine cellar cut into the hill, a tasting room with original beadboard, and a fifth bedroom set up as a guest suite. A detached studio above the three-car garage would make a graceful office, an in-law unit, or — as the current owners use it — a painter's room.

The land is the rare thing. The bench above the home is terraced and irrigated, ready for syrah, viognier, or a small orchard. Eight zoned irrigation circuits. Two seasonal creeks. And the kind of view — vineyards in three directions, the Sierra on the fourth — that doesn't come on the market often.

— Sara Cooper, Broker. Visited the property May 4.
