Plate II · Three ways to coordinate the move

Two transactions,
one timeline.

Most relocations fail one of three ways — and most agents only know one of them. Toggle the scenarios below to see how each one works.

Cash flow Tight but clean

No bridge financing. Home-sale proceeds wire directly into the foothills close on the same business day. Costs the least; works only when both contracts cooperate.

Stress profile High coordination

Every contingency is leveraged. Inspection days have to line up. Sellers have to agree to a same-day close. We've done this 14 times — it can be done; it requires hands on every clock.

Best for Sellers without bridge access

First-time sellers, sellers without significant cash reserves, sellers who can't carry two mortgages even for a week. The most common scenario we run.

Cash flow ~$3–6K / mo carrying

Bridge loan typically 8.5–10% APR for 6 months. We work with three Calaveras-area lenders who price competitively for cross-county relocations.

Stress profile Move once. Sell empty.

You're already up here when your home shows. An empty home photographs better, open houses are easier to schedule, and there's no choreography around your daily life.

Best for Equity-rich sellers

Sellers with substantial home equity, retirees, anyone with strong reserves who would rather pay for breathing room than gain a few thousand on the sale price.

Cash flow Rent your old home

Buyer typically accepts a 30–60 day rent-back at market rate. You hold your equity in cash until the foothills purchase closes. Average net cost: $4–7K total.

Stress profile Hardest negotiation

Your buyer must agree to the rent-back upfront — strongest in a competitive seller's market, hardest when inventory is loose. We script this into the listing strategy from Day 0.

Best for The patient picker

Buyers who know they want the foothills but don't yet know which town or which house. The rent-back gives you 30–60 days of cash-funded calendar to walk it correctly.

Plate VI · The first conversation

Ten questions
before any house.

A relocation conversation starts with these, not with listings. If you can answer most of them, we can model your timeline within a week.

01

What's your current home worth — honestly?

Not Zillow. Sara pulls comps and walks the property in week one.

02

Mortgage balance, second loans, HELOCs?

This drives bridge eligibility and net-proceeds math.

03

What's your cash floor after both transactions?

Reserves matter more than purchase price. We model three scenarios.

04

Hard move date — or flexible window?

School year, lease end, retirement date — these shape the timeline.

05

Remote-work fiber requirement?

Most foothills towns now have it. A few addresses still don't.

06

Primary, second home, or eventual primary?

Tax, insurance, lender posture all shift on this answer.

07

Land or in-town?

If land, James starts; if in-town, Sara starts.

08

Fire-zone tolerance?

Insurance reality varies wildly. Some parcels can't be insured.

09

Two cars or three? Boat? RV? Horses?

Shapes lot size, garage spec, HOA fit.

10

What does a perfect Saturday look like?

This is the question that picks the town. Hardest to answer; most important.
